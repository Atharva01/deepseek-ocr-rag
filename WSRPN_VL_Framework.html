1. Mean Average Precision (mAP)

Definition:

mAP is the average of Average Precision (AP) values computed across all object classes. It measures how well the detector localizes and classifies pathologies at varying confidence thresholds.

Mathematical Formula:

Average Precision (AP) for class c:
APc = ‚à´‚ÇÄ¬π P(r) dr ‚âà Œ£k (Rk - Rk-1) √ó Pk
Where:
‚Ä¢ P(r) = Precision at recall level r

‚Ä¢ Pk = Precision at rank k

Calculation Components:

Interpretation:

  • Phase 3 (34.3%): +5.2% improvement = better localization precision
  • Why it matters: Primary metric for weakly-supervised detection; measures both classification AND localization accuracy

2. F1 Score (Harmonic Mean)
